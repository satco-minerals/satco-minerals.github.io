  <h1>Oil Well Cement</h1>
  <p>Oil Well Cement is a high-performance hydraulic cement specifically engineered for the critical task of cementing oil and gas wells. It is formulated to maintain its integrity under extreme high-pressure and high-temperature (HPHT) conditions, providing a robust seal between the well casing and the surrounding geological formations.</p>

  <h2>Cement Strength Classes</h2>
  <p>Unlike general construction cement, Oil Well Cement is classified by API (American Petroleum Institute) classes based on the depth and conditions of the well:</p>
  <ul>
    <li><b>Class G (Moderate/High):</b> The most common universal grade, designed for depths up to 8,000 feet. It is highly versatile and can be modified with additives to suit various conditions.</li>
    <li><b>Class H (High):</b> A high-strength grade used for deep wells. It is similar to Class G but has a different specific surface area to accommodate specific thickening time requirements.</li>
    <li><b>Class A/B:</b> Basic grades used for shallow wells where extreme pressure/temperature are not factors.</li>
    <li><b>Class D/E/F:</b> Specialized grades for deep wells characterized by extreme pressure and high temperatures.</li>
  </ul>

  <h2>Key Advantages</h2>
  <ul>
    <li><b>Consistent Thickening Time:</b> Engineered to remain pumpable for extended periods during long-distance casing jobs.</li>
    <li><b>Pressure & Temperature Resistance:</b> Maintains mechanical stability in the harsh subterranean environments of drilling rigs.</li>
    <li><b>Low Fluid Loss:</b> Formulated to prevent the loss of water into porous formations, ensuring a strong bond.</li>
    <li><b>High Chemical Stability:</b> Resists contamination from drilling muds and chemical impurities found in oil reservoirs.</li>
  </ul>

  <h2>Primary Applications</h2>
  <ul>
    <li><b>Casing Cementing:</b> Creating a permanent, impermeable seal between the steel casing and the rock wall.</li>
    <li><b>Plug & Abandonment:</b> Sealing decommissioned wells to prevent environmental contamination and fluid migration.</li>
    <li><b>Squeeze Cementing:</b> Used for remedial repair of existing wells to fix leaks or isolate specific zones.</li>
    <li><b>HPHT Wells:</b> Specialized applications in deep-sea or high-temperature geothermal drilling.</li>
  </ul>
