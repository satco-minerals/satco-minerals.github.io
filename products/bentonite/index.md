  <h1>Bentonite</h1>
  <p>Bentonite is a versatile natural clay, primarily composed of the mineral montmorillonite, a hydrated aluminum silicate with a layered structure. Renowned for its exceptional water absorption, swelling capacity, and binding properties, it serves as a critical mineral across diverse global industries, including oil drilling, construction, agriculture, and water treatment.</p>

  <h2>Key Features & Advantages</h2>
  <ul>
    <li><b>High Swelling Capacity:</b> Especially in sodium-based varieties, bentonite can increase its volume multiple times upon contact with water, making it ideal for soil stabilization and drilling fluids.</li>
    <li><b>Binding & Gelation:</b> Its unique layered structure allows for high-strength gel formation, essential for foundry molding sands and specialized cement formulations.</li>
    <li><b>Ion Exchange Capability:</b> Possesses a high capacity for cation exchange, facilitating its use in soil conditioning and the removal of heavy metals from industrial wastewater.</li>
    <li><b>Chemical Stability:</b> Demonstrates significant resistance to harsh chemical environments, including acids and bases, ensuring reliability in complex industrial processes.</li>
  </ul>

  <h2>Primary Applications</h2>
  <ul>
    <li><b>Oil & Gas Drilling:</b> Serves as a fundamental component of drilling mud, acting as a lubricant, coolant, and stabilizer for wellbore walls.</li>
    <li><b>Environmental & Water Treatment:</b> Utilized as a high-performance filter media to adsorb contaminants, heavy metals, and toxins from industrial effluent and polluted soil.</li>
    <li><b>Agriculture:</b> Acts as a soil conditioner, increasing water retention capacity and improving nutrient exchange, while also serving as a carrier for fertilizers.</li>
    <li><b>Foundry Industry:</b> Functions as a high-performance binder in the production of precision sand molds for metal casting.</li>
    <li><b>Construction:</b> Essential in infrastructure projects such as dam and tunnel waterproofing, as well as soil stabilization.</li>
    <li><b>Pharmaceuticals & Cosmetics:</b> Used for its absorbent properties in tablet production and facial skincare products.</li>
  </ul>

  <h2>Types of Bentonites</h2>
  <ul>
    <li><b>Sodium Bentonite:</b> Characterized by superior swelling and water absorption, making it the preferred choice for drilling and geotechnical applications.</li>
    <li><b>Calcium Bentonite:</b> Features lower swelling properties but retains excellent bonding and absorbent capabilities, widely used in purification, agricultural, and pharmaceutical sectors.</li>
  </ul>
