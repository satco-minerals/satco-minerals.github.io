Portland-Pozzolan Cement (PPC): Durability & Resilience:
Portland-Pozzolan Cement is a blended hydraulic cement produced by inter-grinding Portland cement clinker with high-quality pozzolanic materials. This formulation is engineered to enhance long-term durability, reduce permeability, and improve resistance to chemical attacks, making it an excellent choice for environmental sustainability and aggressive site conditions.

Cement Strength Classes:
These classes represent the minimum compressive strength in kilograms per square centimeter ($kg/cm^2$) at 28 days, ensuring performance in both standard and specialized applications:
	P-325: Used for general masonry, residential construction, and non-structural elements where moderate strength and enhanced durability are preferred.
	P-425: The standard structural grade, widely used for foundations, marine structures, and reinforced concrete in aggressive environments.

Key Advantages:
	Enhanced Chemical Resistance: Superior performance in sulfate-rich environments and against chloride penetration.
	Reduced Permeability: Creates a denser concrete matrix, preventing the ingress of harmful agents.
	Controlled Heat of Hydration: Minimizes the risk of thermal cracking in massive structures.
	Environmental Sustainability: Utilizes industrial by-products, significantly reducing the carbon footprint compared to standard cement.

Primary Applications:
	Massive Concrete Structures: Ideal for dams, bridge piers, and foundations where thermal management is vital.
	Marine & Coastal Construction: Provides high resistance to salt-water erosion and sulfate attack.
	General Structural Concrete: Suitable for columns, slabs, and walls in residential and industrial projects.
	Aggressive Soil Conditions: Recommended for foundations in regions with high sulfate or acidic groundwater.
