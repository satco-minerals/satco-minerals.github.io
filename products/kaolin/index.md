  <h1>Kaolin (Al₂Si₂O₅(OH)₄)</h1>
  <p>Kaolin, also known as China clay, is a versatile industrial mineral characterized by its fine particle size, white color, and excellent chemical inertness. It is an essential raw material in various industries requiring high-quality fillers, coatings, and structural components.</p>

  <h2>Key Properties</h2>
  <ul>
    <li><b>High Whiteness/Brightness:</b> Ideal for paper and paint applications.</li>
    <li><b>Fine Particle Size:</b> Enhances smoothness and opacity in coatings.</li>
    <li><b>Chemical Stability:</b> Excellent resistance to acidic and alkaline environments.</li>
    <li><b>Low Conductivity:</b> High electrical resistance for insulating applications.</li>
  </ul>

  <h2>Advantages & Disadvantages</h2>
  <ul>
    <li><b>Advantages:</b> Excellent plasticity when wet; high covering power in paints and coatings.</li>
    <li><b>Disadvantages:</b> Sensitive to iron contamination, which can reduce brightness; requires precise drying to prevent clumping.</li>
  </ul>

  <h2>Applications</h2>
  <ul>
    <li><b>Ceramics:</b> Primary component for high-quality porcelain, tableware, and sanitaryware.</li>
    <li><b>Paper Industry:</b> Used as a filler and coating pigment to improve paper surface and brightness.</li>
    <li><b>Paints & Coatings:</b> Functions as an extender and functional filler to enhance durability.</li>
    <li><b>Rubber & Plastics:</b> Acts as a reinforcing filler to improve mechanical properties.</li>
  </ul>
