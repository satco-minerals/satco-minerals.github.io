  <h1>Micro Silica (Silica Fume)</h1>
  <p>Micro silica, or silica fume, is an ultrafine, amorphous powder generated as a by-product of ferrosilicon alloy production in electric arc furnaces. With over 90% non-crystalline silica and an average particle diameter of 0.1 microns, it serves as a highly active pozzolanic material essential for enhancing concrete durability and mechanical properties.</p>

  <h2>Key Features & Advantages</h2>
  <ul>
    <li><b>Superior Pozzolanic Reaction:</b> Its high purity and fineness allow it to react rapidly with hydrated lime in cement paste to form insoluble calcium silicate, densifying the concrete structure.</li>
    <li><b>Reduced Permeability:</b> It significantly reduces permeability to water, chloride ions, and sulfates, effectively delaying the onset and rate of rebar corrosion.</li>
    <li><b>Enhanced Mechanical Strength:</b> The additive increases compressive, flexural, and tensile strength, while also improving resistance to abrasion and freeze-thaw cycles.</li>
    <li><b>Versatile Forms:</b> Beyond standard powder, it is available as a slurry or as "Dezomix 4500" gel, which helps reduce plastic cracks and improves performance under dynamic loads.</li>
  </ul>

  <h2>Primary Applications</h2>
  <ul>
    <li><b>Harsh Environments:</b> Ideal for coastal structures, piers, bridges, and concrete exposed to severe sulfate attacks.</li>
    <li><b>Heavy-Duty Construction:</b> Used in the production of high-strength concrete for dams, tunnels, water reservoirs, and canals.</li>
    <li><b>Specialized Infrastructure:</b> Suitable for flooring, architectural facades, and military or defense-related concrete structures.</li>
  </ul>
