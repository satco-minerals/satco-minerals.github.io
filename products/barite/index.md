  <h1>Barite (BaSO₄)</h1>
  <p>Barite is a dense, non-metallic mineral composed primarily of barium sulfate. Due to its exceptionally high specific gravity and chemical inertness, it is the industry standard weighting agent for drilling fluids, ensuring safety and efficiency in oil and gas exploration.</p>

  <h2>Key Properties</h2>
  <ul>
    <li><b>High Specific Gravity:</b> Typically ranges from 4.10 to 4.30+, providing the density required to control downhole pressure.</li>
    <li><b>Chemical Inertness:</b> Does not react with drilling fluids or reservoir formations.</li>
    <li><b>Low Abrasion:</b> Minimizes wear on drilling equipment.</li>
  </ul>

  <h2>Advantages & Disadvantages</h2>
  <ul>
    <li><b>Advantages:</b> Essential for controlling high-pressure oil/gas wells; environmentally safe and non-toxic.</li>
    <li><b>Disadvantages:</b> Requires high-precision grinding to meet particle size distribution (PSD) standards for drilling fluids.</li>
  </ul>

  <h2>Applications</h2>
  <ul>
    <li><b>Oil & Gas Drilling:</b> Primary weighting agent in drilling muds.</li>
    <li><b>Chemical Industry:</b> Source of barium for various chemical applications.</li>
    <li><b>Radiation Shielding:</b> Used in high-density concrete for medical and research facilities.</li>
    <li><b>Fillers:</b> Utilized in paints, plastics, and rubber industries.</li>
  </ul>
