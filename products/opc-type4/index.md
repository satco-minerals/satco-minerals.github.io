  <h1>Portland Cement Type 4: Low Heat of Hydration</h1>
  <p>Portland Cement Type 4 is specifically formulated for massive concrete structures where minimizing the heat generated during hydration is critical. By limiting temperature rise, it effectively prevents thermal cracking in large-scale pours, ensuring structural stability and durability in massive elements.</p>

  <h2>Key Advantages</h2>
  <ul>
    <li><b>Low Heat of Hydration:</b> Minimizes internal thermal stress in massive concrete sections.</li>
    <li><b>Thermal Crack Control:</b> Significantly reduces the risk of cracks caused by temperature differentials.</li>
    <li><b>Dimensional Stability:</b> Provides long-term structural integrity for large-scale engineering works.</li>
    <li><b>Controlled Curing:</b> Allows for a slower, more uniform strength gain, ideal for specialized applications.</li>
  </ul>

  <h2>Primary Applications</h2>
  <ul>
    <li><b>Mass Concrete Structures:</b> Essential for dams, large piers, and massive bridge foundations.</li>
    <li><b>Thick Foundations:</b> Used in deep and extensive foundation systems where heat accumulation is a concern.</li>
    <li><b>Large Retaining Walls:</b> Ideal for structures with high volume-to-surface-area ratios.</li>
    <li><b>Industrial Infrastructure:</b> Reliable for heavy-duty projects requiring extreme durability.</li>
  </ul>
