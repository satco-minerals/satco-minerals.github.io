  <h1>Portland Cement Type 2: Moderate Sulfate Resistance & Controlled Heat</h1>
  <p>Portland Cement Type 2 is a moderate-heat, sulfate-resistant cement designed for structures where protection against moderate sulfate attack is required. It is an ideal choice for construction in soils or groundwater containing moderate concentrations of sulfates, as well as for structures where a lower heat of hydration is beneficial to prevent thermal cracking.</p>

  <h2>Portland Cement Type 2 Strength Classes</h2>
  <p>Type 2 Portland cement, known for its moderate sulfate resistance and controlled heat of hydration, is also produced in various strength classes. These grades indicate the compressive strength in kilograms per square centimeter (kg/cm²) at 28 days, ensuring structural performance in environments exposed to moderate chemical attack.</p>

  <h3>Available Strength Classes for Type 2:</h3>
  <ul>
    <li><b>2-325:</b> Suitable for moderate-exposure environments where standard structural strength is required for general infrastructure.</li>
    <li><b>2-425:</b> The benchmark grade for foundation work, retaining walls, and hydraulic structures in sulfate-bearing soils.</li>
    <li><b>2-525:</b> A high-performance grade utilized for massive concrete elements that require both sulfate resistance and superior early load-bearing capacity.</li>
  </ul>

  <h2>Key Advantages</h2>
  <ul>
    <li><b>Moderate Sulfate Resistance:</b> Provides enhanced protection against sulfate-bearing soils and water.</li>
    <li><b>Controlled Heat of Hydration:</b> Reduces the risk of thermal cracking in moderately thick concrete sections.</li>
    <li><b>Balanced Performance:</b> Offers reliable setting times and consistent strength gain for structural applications.</li>
    <li><b>Durability:</b> Enhances the long-term integrity of concrete in challenging environments.</li>
  </ul>

  <h2>Primary Applications</h2>
  <ul>
    <li><b>Hydraulic Structures:</b> Suitable for piers, abutments, and retaining walls exposed to groundwater.</li>
    <li><b>Foundations in Sulfate-Rich Soils:</b> Ideal for building foundations in areas with moderate sulfate levels.</li>
    <li><b>Mass Concrete:</b> Used in structures where moderate heat generation is necessary to control internal stresses.</li>
    <li><b>General Construction:</b> A dependable option for versatile infrastructure and industrial projects.</li>
  </ul>
