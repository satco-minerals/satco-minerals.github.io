  <h1>Portland Cement Type 5: High Sulfate Resistance</h1>
  <p>Portland Cement Type 5 is specially engineered for projects exposed to severe sulfate environments. It provides maximum protection against sulfate-bearing soils and groundwater, ensuring the long-term durability and structural integrity of concrete in aggressive chemical conditions.</p>

  <h2>Portland Cement Type 5 Strength Classes</h2>
  <p>Type 5 Portland cement is specifically formulated for high resistance to sulfate attack, making it essential for structures in contact with sulfate-rich soils or groundwater. Like other types, it is produced in specific strength classes, measured in kilograms per square centimeter (kg/cm²) at 28 days, to ensure durability in harsh chemical environments.</p>

  <h3>Available Strength Classes for Type 5:</h3>
  <ul>
    <li><b>5-325:</b> Used for foundations, pipes, and light structures in environments with moderate to high sulfate levels.</li>
    <li><b>5-425:</b> The standard structural grade for aggressive environments, providing a balance of chemical resistance and reliable load-bearing capacity.</li>
  </ul>

  <h2>Key Advantages</h2>
  <ul>
    <li><b>High Sulfate Resistance:</b> Offers superior protection against chemical attack in highly sulfate-rich environments.</li>
    <li><b>Enhanced Durability:</b> Increases the lifespan of concrete structures exposed to harsh subsurface conditions.</li>
    <li><b>Structural Reliability:</b> Maintains performance and consistency in challenging, corrosive settings.</li>
    <li><b>Chemical Stability:</b> Minimizes deterioration caused by reactions with external sulfate sources.</li>
  </ul>

  <h2>Primary Applications</h2>
  <ul>
    <li><b>Coastal & Marine Structures:</b> Ideal for harbors, seawalls, and submerged foundations.</li>
    <li><b>Sulfate-Rich Soil Foundations:</b> Essential for construction in areas with high sulfate concentrations.</li>
    <li><b>Sewage & Wastewater Facilities:</b> Suitable for pipes, tanks, and treatment infrastructure exposed to chemical effluents.</li>
    <li><b>Infrastructure in Aggressive Environments:</b> Recommended for heavy-duty civil works where long-term durability is the priority.</li>
  </ul>
