  <h1>Coal (Anthracite & Bituminous)</h1>
  <p>Coal is a vital global energy source and a fundamental reducing agent in metallurgical processes. We supply high-quality coal, categorized by its rank and carbon content, to meet specific industrial, thermal, and metallurgical requirements.</p>

  <h2>A. Anthracite Coal</h2>
  <p>Anthracite is the highest rank of coal, characterized by its hard, glossy appearance, high carbon content, and low impurity profile. It burns with high thermal efficiency and minimal smoke.</p>
  <ul>
    <li><b>Key Properties:</b> High Fixed Carbon (maximum heat energy), Low Volatile Matter (steady, clean combustion), and High Density (structural integrity).</li>
    <li><b>Advantages:</b> Cleanest burning; excellent for high-temperature applications.</li>
    <li><b>Disadvantages:</b> Harder to ignite compared to lower-rank coals; higher cost.</li>
    <li><b>Applications:</b> Steelmaking (carbon additive), water filtration, and high-efficiency home heating.</li>
  </ul>

  <h2>B. Bituminous Coal</h2>
  <p>Bituminous coal is a versatile mid-rank coal, widely used in power generation and as a coking coal in blast furnaces due to its excellent heating value and coking properties.</p>
  <ul>
    <li><b>Key Properties:</b> High Heating Value (large-scale boilers), Coking Potential (plastic upon heating), and Moderate Volatile Matter (flame stability).</li>
    <li><b>Advantages:</b> Versatile; high availability; highly effective for energy production and metallurgy.</li>
    <li><b>Disadvantages:</b> Higher volatile matter compared to anthracite; produces more particulate emissions if not processed.</li>
    <li><b>Applications:</b> Power generation (thermal coal), coking plants, and cement manufacturing.</li>
  </ul>
