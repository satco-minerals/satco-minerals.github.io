  <h1>Portland Composite Cement (PCC)</h1>
  <p>Portland Composite Cement is a high-performance hydraulic binder that hardens through a hydration reaction when mixed with water. Produced by intergrading Portland cement clinker with carefully selected mineral admixtures (such as limestone, pozzolan, or slag), this cement plays a pivotal role in modern construction.</p>

  <h2>Key Features and Benefits</h2>
  <ul>
    <li><b>Enhanced Durability:</b> Provides superior waterproofing and reduced permeability.</li>
    <li><b>Improved Workability:</b> Offers excellent flowability and pumpability, making it easier to place and finish.</li>
    <li><b>High Ultimate Strength:</b> Achieves high long-term compressive strength.</li>
    <li><b>Optimal Performance:</b> Requires lower water demand while maintaining excellent water retention in the cement paste.</li>
    <li><b>Superior Chemical Resistance:</b> Highly resistant to aggressive chemical environments, particularly sulfate attacks in seawater.</li>
    <li><b>Reduced Efflorescence:</b> Effectively minimizes salt deposits (efflorescence) on concrete surfaces.</li>
    <li><b>Enhanced Bonding:</b> Improves adhesion between concrete components and steel reinforcement.</li>
  </ul>

  <h2>Primary Applications & Limitations</h2>
  <p>While highly versatile, it is recommended to exercise caution when using this cement in cold weather or freezing conditions. For specific applications like decorative facade work or tile installation, it is considered the second preference after Type I Portland cement.</p>
